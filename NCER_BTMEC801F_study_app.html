Unit-wise syllabus

Unit 1 — Introduction & Solar Cells
CO-01 · 6–8 Lectures
  • World energy scenario — fossil fuel crisis, need for non-conventional sources
  • Classification of energy resources — renewable vs non-renewable
  • India's energy policy — MNRE, IREDA, CASE (1981)
  • Solar cell theory — p-n junction, photovoltaic effect, band diagram
  • Solar cell materials, arrays, power plants, limitations
  • Czochralski process, zone refining for silicon crystal growth
  • PV water pumping system
Unit 2 — Solar Thermal Energy
CO-02 · 6–8 Lectures
  • Solar radiation geometry — zenith angle, azimuth, hour angle, declination, solar constant
  • Solar spectrum, clarity index, day length hours
  • Flat plate collectors — liquid & air heaters, materials, performance
  • Evacuated tube collectors — principle, working
  • Focusing/concentrating collectors — CSP systems
  • Solar thermal power plants, solar heating & cooling
Unit 3 — Wind & Ocean Energy
CO-02 · 6–8 Lectures
  • Wind energy — sources, data, energy estimation
  • Wind machine parameters — cut-in, cut-out, rated speed; tip speed ratio; Betz limit
  • Rotor types — horizontal axis (HAWT) vs vertical axis (VAWT)
  • OTEC (Ocean Thermal Energy Conversion) — open & closed cycle
  • Wave & tidal energy principles
Unit 4 — Biomass & Geothermal Energy
CO-03 · 6–8 Lectures
  • Biomass — definition, feedstocks, energy crops
  • Conversion processes — combustion, anaerobic digestion (biogas), gasification, fermentation
  • Biogas — composition, production, advantages/disadvantages
  • Geothermal energy — resources, classification
  • Geothermal systems — dry steam, flash steam, binary cycle, EGS
  • Geothermal heat pump; environmental considerations
Unit 5 — Energy Storage: Batteries & Fuel Cells
CO-04 · 6–8 Lectures
  • Batteries — primary vs secondary, parts and functions
  • Lead-acid (Pb-A), Ni-Zn, Lithium-ion batteries — construction & working
  • C-rate, charge-discharge curves, polarization curves
  • Fuel cells — principle, components, types (PEMFC, AFC, PAFC, MCFC, SOFC)
  • Fuel cell polarization — activation, ohmic, concentration losses
  • PV cell — operating principle (revisited for energy storage context)
Unit 6 — Advanced Storage & Emerging Technologies
CO-05 · 6–8 Lectures
  • Flywheel energy storage — principle, construction, advantages
  • Supercapacitors — construction, working, comparison with flywheels
  • MHD (Magnetohydrodynamic) power generation — principle, working
  • MHD challenges and limitations
  • Thermoelectric & thermionic energy conversion basics

Exam pattern (ESE) — DBATU BTMEC801F
Total: 60 marks · Duration: 3 hrs 45 min
  • Q1 — MCQ (12 marks): 12 objective questions × 1 mark each. All compulsory. Covers all 6 units.
  • Q2 to Q6 — Descriptive (48 marks): Each question has 3 sub-parts (A/B/C), attempt any 2. Each sub-part = 6 marks. One question per unit roughly.
  • Scientific non-programmable calculator allowed.
  • Assume suitable data and state assumptions clearly.
CA / MSE pattern (Internal)
College level assessment
  • MCQ section: 1 mark each (CO-01 to CO-05)
  • Descriptive: 5 marks each (short answer) or 6 marks each (long answer)
  • Questions usually tied to specific COs (CO-01 to CO-05)
